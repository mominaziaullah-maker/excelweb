Calculation Formulas

Full Load Amps (FLA)

Amperes
FLA = (HP × 746) / (V × Eff × PF × √3)

Calculates the continuous current drawn by the motor during normal operating conditions at full load.

Locked Rotor Amps (LRA)

Amperes
LRA = FLA × LRA Code Multiplier

The maximum current drawn by the motor during start-up when the rotor is stationary (locked).

Short Circuit MVA (MVAsc)

MVA
MVAsc = (Isc × √3 × kV) / 1000

The available short-circuit capacity of the utility/transformer at the point of connection.

Voltage Drop % (VD)

Percentage (%)
VD% = [LR MVA / (LR MVA + MVAsc)] × 100

The temporary voltage drop (sag) at the system busbar during motor start-up.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is LRA Code Multiplier?

NEMA standards specify letter codes (A to V) on motor nameplates that represent the starting kVA per HP. If you don't know the code, a standard industry default starting multiplier is 6.0 (meaning starting current is 6 times the normal FLA).

How does RVAT Tap % affect Voltage Drop?

A Reduced Voltage Auto-Transformer (RVAT) starter reduces the starting voltage at the motor terminals. For example, using an 80% tap reduces starting current to 64% ($(0.8)^2$) and significantly lowers the system voltage drop.

What is Available SC Isc?

It is the maximum prospective Short Circuit Current (in Amperes) that the power supply network can deliver at your installation bus. A higher short-circuit capacity means a "stronger" grid with less voltage drop.
